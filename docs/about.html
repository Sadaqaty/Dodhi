OUR STORY

Built for the people who feed us

Dodhi started from a simple observation: milkmen do vital work but have no tools built for them.

The problem

In Pakistan, millions of families depend on local milkmen — known as Dodhis — who deliver fresh milk every morning and evening. These milkmen keep track of who got how much milk and who paid, usually on paper slips or in notebooks.

The problem? Paper gets wet, lost, torn. Memory fails. At the end of the month, there's confusion about money. Customers and milkmen argue about how much was delivered. There's no history, no proof, no reports.

We wanted to fix that — without making things complicated.

"صاف حساب، لمبی یاری"

A clean ledger leads to a long partnership. This old saying is the heart of Dodhi. When accounts are clear, relationships stay strong.

What we built

Dodhi is a simple Android app that replaces paper records with a digital system. A milkman opens the app, taps who got milk and who didn't, and the app handles the rest — billing, balances, reports.

It works in both English and Urdu because the people using it live in Pakistan, not Silicon Valley. It works offline because internet isn't always available in rural areas. And it sends reports via WhatsApp because that's how people communicate.

The app also backs up data automatically to Google Drive, so even if the phone is lost, the data isn't.
